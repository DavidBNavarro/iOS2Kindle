Web2Kindle — Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 3, 2026

Web2Kindle is a Chrome extension that converts web articles into EPUB format and sends them to your Kindle. This privacy policy explains what data the extension handles and how.

Data stored on your device

All of the following data is stored locally in your browser's storage and never leaves your device unless explicitly described below:

  • Kindle email address — the email address you configure for sending EPUBs.
  • License key — your Web2Kindle Pro license key, if purchased.
  • Conversion history — up to 500 recent entries containing article titles and URLs you have converted.
  • Preferences — settings like whether to include images or links, paywall bypass configuration, and server URL.

Data sent externally

The extension sends data to external services only when you perform specific actions:

  • Google Gmail API — when you click "Send to Kindle," the extension uses your Google account (via OAuth) to send the generated EPUB as an email attachment to your Kindle address. Your Gmail address is retrieved to populate the "From" field. No other Gmail data is accessed.
  • Archive services (archive.org, archive.ph, etc.) — when processing a URL from a configured paywalled site, the article URL is sent to an archive service to retrieve a cached copy. This is opt-in and configurable.
  • Article and image servers — when converting an article, the extension fetches the page HTML and embedded images from their original servers. This is the core functionality of the extension.
  • License verification — when you verify a license key, only the key string is sent to web2kindle-verify.vercel.app. No email, name, or other personal information is transmitted.

What we do not do

  • No analytics, telemetry, or usage tracking.
  • No data is sent to the extension developer's servers (the license verification endpoint receives only your license key).
  • No content scripts are injected into web pages automatically.
  • No browser history is accessed.
  • No data is sold, shared with third parties, or used for advertising.

Permissions

  • activeTab — reads the current tab's content only when you open the extension popup.
  • scripting — reads page HTML as a fallback when direct fetch fails.
  • storage — stores your settings and history locally.
  • identity — authenticates with Google to send emails via the Gmail API.
  • contextMenus — adds right-click menu items for quick access.
  • <all_urls> — allows fetching article content and images from any website you choose to convert.
